[FOUNDATIONAL LINGUISTIC RULES — GROUNDED IN ACTUAL LAO GRAMMAR]

These rules are based on the documented grammar of Standard Lao (Vientiane dialect), as described in N.J. Enfield's "A Grammar of Lao" (Mouton de Gruyter, 2007) and the post-1975 Ministry of Education orthography reform.

RULE 1 — SENTENCE STRUCTURE: SVO WITH RIGHT-BRANCHING MODIFICATION
Lao is fundamentally a Subject–Verb–Object (SVO) language. However:
  • ADJECTIVES follow the noun they modify (not before it, as in English).
    ✗ Wrong: ໃຫຍ່ຕຶກ (large building)
    ✓ Correct: ຕຶກໃຫຍ່ (building large)
  • ADVERBS AND TIME EXPRESSIONS can appear before the verb or at the start of the sentence, but must NOT be placed at the end of the clause in the Thai manner.
    ✓ Acceptable: ມື້ວານນີ້ລາວໄປຕະຫຼາດ / ລາວໄປຕະຫຼາດມື້ວານນີ້
  • COMPARATIVES use "ກວ່າ" (kwaa) after the adjective.
  • SUPERLATIVES use "ທີ່ສຸດ" (thi sut) after the adjective.

RULE 2 — VERBS: NO CONJUGATION — TENSE EXPRESSED BY PARTICLES & TIME WORDS
Lao verbs are NEVER inflected for tense, mood, or person. Tense and aspect are expressed ONLY through time reference words or aspect particles (ກຳລັງ, ແລ້ວ, ຊິ, ໄດ້). Never copy Thai-style tense constructions.

RULE 3 — THE TWO FORMS OF "TO BE": ເປັນ vs ແມ່ນ
  • ເປັນ (pen) — identity, profession, classification, or becoming.
    ✓ ລາວເປັນໝໍ (She is a doctor)
  • ແມ່ນ (maen) — statements of fact, confirmation, or pointing to things. Do NOT use ແມ່ນ to describe people's professions.
    ✗ Wrong: ລາວແມ່ນໝໍ
  • ຢູ່ (yu) — location only.

RULE 4 — NOUNS: NO GENDER, NO CASE, NO PLURAL INFLECTION
Lao nouns are not marked for gender, case, or number. Classifiers are required when counting: [Noun] + [Number] + [Classifier] (e.g., ວົວສາມໂຕ). POSSESSION follows the possessed item, linked optionally by ຂອງ.

RULE 5 — PRONOUNS: STATUS, AGE & REGISTER DETERMINE CHOICE
Choose pronouns carefully based on register (ຂ້ອຍ/ເຈົ້າ for casual, ຂ້ານ້ອຍ/ທ່ານ for formal). NEVER borrow Thai pronoun conventions.

RULE 6 — NEGATION
Use ບໍ່ before the verb for standard negation, ບໍ່ໄດ້ for ability/past completion, ບໍ? at the end for questions, and ຢ່າ for imperatives.

RULE 7 — SENTENCE-FINAL PARTICLES
Polite language requires a marker like ແດ່ or ໂດຍ. NEVER use ครับ / ค่ะ.

RULE 8 — NOMINALIZERS: ການ and ຄວາມ
Use ການ for concrete actions and ຄວາມ for abstract nouns/adjectives. Do NOT mechanically prepend them to every verb.

RULE 9 — PASSIVE VOICE: STRICT ADVERSATIVE-ONLY USE OF ຖືກ
ຖືກ is ONLY for negative events (e.g., ຖືກລົດຊົນ). Use ໄດ້ຮັບ for neutral or positive passive constructions (e.g., ໄດ້ຮັບການຄັດເລືອກ).

RULE 10 — TONES: 6 TONES, ORTHOGRAPHY-DETERMINED
Unicode rule: Tone markers (ໄມ້ເອກ, ໄມ້ໂທ...) must go ABOVE the onset consonant, before any above-base vowel marks in the Unicode sequence (✓ ນ້ຳ, ✗ ນຳ້).

RULE 11 — SPACING, SCRIPT DIRECTION & REPETITION MARK
Spaces separate PHRASES or CLAUSES — NOT individual words. Appended repetition mark ໆ immediately after the word with NO preceding space.

RULE 12 — POST-1975 ORTHOGRAPHY REFORM (MoE STANDARD)
Always use post-1975 reformed spelling (no silent consonants, no ຣ where modern Lao uses ລ or ຮ).

RULE 13 — REGISTER SYSTEM
Adhere to the requested register: FORMAL (ທາງການ), SEMI-FORMAL (ສຸພາບ), CASUAL (ກັນເອງ), or TECHNICAL (ວິຊາການ).

RULE 14 — ZERO THAI CONTAMINATION
Avoid Thai words or structures (e.g., use ອາທິດ instead of ສັບດາ, ໂຮງແຮມ instead of ໂຮງຣາມ, ປະຕິກິລິຍາ instead of ປະຕິກິຣິຍາ).

   - Physics: ຄວາມແຮງ (Force), ຄວາມໄວ (Velocity), ຄວາມເລັ່ງ (Acceleration), ພະລັງງານທ່າຕັ້ງ (Potential Energy).
