[FOUNDATIONAL LINGUISTIC RULES — GROUNDED IN ACTUAL LAO GRAMMAR]


These rules are based on the documented grammar of Standard Lao (Vientiane dialect),
as described in N.J. Enfield's "A Grammar of Lao" (Mouton de Gruyter, 2007) and the
post-1975 Ministry of Education orthography reform.


RULE 1 — SENTENCE STRUCTURE: SVO WITH RIGHT-BRANCHING MODIFICATION

Lao is fundamentally a Subject–Verb–Object (SVO) language. However:

  • ADJECTIVES follow the noun they modify (not before it, as in English).
    ✗ Wrong: ໃຫຍ່ຕຶກ (large building) — adjective before noun (English order)
    ✓ Correct: ຕຶກໃຫຍ່ (building large)

  • ADVERBS AND TIME EXPRESSIONS can appear before the verb or at the start of
    the sentence, but must NOT be placed at the end of the clause in the Thai manner.
    ✓ Acceptable: ມື້ວານນີ້ລາວໄປຕະຫຼາດ / ລາວໄປຕະຫຼາດມື້ວານນີ້

  • COMPARATIVES use "ກວ່າ" (kwaa) after the adjective:
    ✓ ລາວສູງກວ່າຂ້ອຍ (She is taller than me)

  • SUPERLATIVES use "ທີ່ສຸດ" (thi sut) after the adjective:
    ✓ ລາວສູງທີ່ສຸດ (She is the tallest)

  • For emphasis, the object may move to the beginning of the sentence, but this is
    a stylistic choice, not the default.


RULE 2 — VERBS: NO CONJUGATION — TENSE EXPRESSED BY PARTICLES & TIME WORDS

Lao verbs are NEVER inflected for tense, mood, or person. Never add suffixes or
modify the verb stem. Tense and aspect are expressed ONLY through:

  a) Time reference words: ມື້ວານ (yesterday), ມື້ອື່ນ (tomorrow), ຕອນນີ້ (now), etc.

  b) Aspect particles placed in specific positions:
     • ກຳລັງ (kamlang) — BEFORE verb → progressive
       ✓ ລາວກຳລັງກິນເຂົ້າ (She is eating)
     • ແລ້ວ (laew) — AFTER verb or at sentence end → completed action
       ✓ ລາວກິນເຂົ້າແລ້ວ (She has already eaten)
     • ຊິ (si) — BEFORE verb → future intention
       ✓ ລາວຊິໄປ (She will go)
     • ໄດ້ (dai) — BEFORE verb → ability or past completion (often in negatives)
       ✓ ລາວໄດ້ໄປແລ້ວ / ລາວໄປບໍ່ໄດ້

  RULE: Never copy Thai-style tense constructions into the Lao verb phrase.


RULE 3 — THE TWO FORMS OF "TO BE": ເປັນ vs ແມ່ນ

  • ເປັນ (pen) — identity, profession, classification, or becoming.
    ✓ ລາວເປັນໝໍ (She is a doctor)

  • ແມ່ນ (maen) — statements of fact, confirmation, or pointing to things.
    Do NOT use ແມ່ນ to describe people's professions or attributes.
    ✓ ນັ້ນແມ່ນລົດຂອງຂ້ອຍ (That is my car)
    ✗ Wrong: ລາວແມ່ນໝໍ — common Thai contamination error.

  • ຢູ່ (yu) — location only.
    ✓ ລາວຢູ່ເຮືອນ (She is at home)


RULE 4 — NOUNS: NO GENDER, NO CASE, NO PLURAL INFLECTION

Lao nouns are not marked for gender, case, or number. Plurality is expressed by
context, quantity words, or classifiers. Classifiers are required when counting:

  Structure: [Noun] + [Number] + [Classifier]
  Common classifiers:
  • ຄົນ — people  • ໂຕ — animals  • ອັນ — small objects
  • ຫຼັງ — buildings  • ອາດ — flat items  • ກໍລະນີ — cases (formal/technical)

  ✓ ວົວສາມໂຕ (three cattle)   ✓ ຄົນສອງຄົນ (two people)

  POSSESSION: Possessor follows the possessed item, linked optionally by ຂອງ:
  ✓ ລົດຂ້ອຍ or ລົດຂອງຂ້ອຍ (my car)


RULE 5 — PRONOUNS: STATUS, AGE & REGISTER DETERMINE CHOICE

  FIRST PERSON:
  • ຂ້ອຍ — common everyday  • ຂ້ານ້ອຍ — formal/deferential  • ເຮົາ — informal/we

  SECOND PERSON:
  • ເຈົ້າ — standard polite  • ທ່ານ — very formal (officials, elders)  • ສູ — very informal

  THIRD PERSON:
  • ເຂົາ — formal/general  • ລາວ — very informal  • ເພິ່ນ — very formal
  • ມັນ — objects/animals ONLY; offensive if used for a person

  PLURAL: Add ພວກ before pronoun → ພວກເຈົ້າ (you all), ພວກເຮົາ (we)

  RULE: In formal writing always use ຂ້ານ້ອຍ / ທ່ານ and retain the pronoun explicitly.
  In informal Lao, pronouns may be dropped when subject is clear from context.
  NEVER borrow Thai pronoun conventions.


RULE 6 — NEGATION

  • ບໍ່ (bo) — before the verb → standard negation
    ✓ ຂ້ອຍບໍ່ໄປ (I am not going)
  • ບໍ່ໄດ້ (bo dai) — negates ability or past completion
    ✓ ຂ້ອຍໄປບໍ່ໄດ້ (I cannot go)
  • ບໍ? at sentence end — yes/no question
    ✓ ລາວໄປບໍ? (Is she going?)
  • ຢ່າ (ya) — negative imperative
    ✓ ຢ່າເວົ້າດັງ (Don't speak loudly)


RULE 7 — SENTENCE-FINAL PARTICLES

Polite language requires a politeness marker at the end of every phrase in
formal speech. Three classes:

  POLITENESS / SOFTENING:
  • ແດ່ (dae) — softens statements, requests, imperatives; most common all-purpose
    particle. Use freely in professional and formal writing.
    ✓ ກະລຸນາຊ່ວຍຂ້ານ້ອຍແດ່ (Please help me)
  • ເດີ / ເດີ້ (doe) — more emphatic than ແດ່; adds urgency; less polite.

  QUESTION:
  • ບໍ (bo) — neutral yes/no question
  • ບໍ່ລ (bola) — yes/no question where speaker expects a positive answer

  RULE: NEVER use ครับ / ค่ะ. These Thai particles have no place in Lao.


RULE 8 — NOMINALIZERS: ການ and ຄວາມ

  • ການ + [action verb] → concrete action as noun
    ✓ ເດີນທາງ → ການເດີນທາງ (travel)
  • ຄວາມ + [abstract verb or adjective] → abstract noun
    ✓ ຄິດ → ຄວາມຄິດ (thought)   ✓ ດີ → ຄວາມດີ (goodness)

  WARNING: Do NOT mechanically prepend ການ / ຄວາມ to every verb as Thai does.
  In natural Lao, verbs can stand as nouns through context. Overuse is a
  hallmark of Thai-contaminated writing.


RULE 9 — PASSIVE VOICE: STRICT ADVERSATIVE-ONLY USE OF ຖືກ

ຖືກ (theuk) is ONLY for adversative/negative events — something bad happened
TO the subject.

  ✓ ລາວຖືກລົດຊົນ (He was hit by a car — bad event)
  ✗ ລາວຖືກເລືອກເປັນຫົວໜ້າ — implies he suffered it!
  ✓ ລາວໄດ້ຮັບການເລືອກເປັນຫົວໜ້າ (He was selected as leader — neutral/positive)

  Use ໄດ້ຮັບ for all neutral or positive passive constructions.


RULE 10 — TONES: 6 TONES, ORTHOGRAPHY-DETERMINED

Vientiane Lao has SIX tones: Low, Mid, High, Rising, High Rising, Low Falling.
Tone of a syllable is determined by THREE factors working together:
  1. Consonant class (high / mid / low class)
  2. Syllable type (open/unchecked vs. closed/checked)
  3. Tone marker present: ໄມ້ເອກ, ໄມ້ໂທ, ໄມ້ຕີ, ໄມ້ຈັດຕະວາ

  UNICODE RULE: Tone markers go ABOVE the onset consonant, before any above-base
  vowel marks in the Unicode sequence.
  ✓ Correct: ນ້ຳ, ເຮົ້າ, ຢ່າ
  ✗ Wrong: ນຳ້ (vowel before tone mark — misrenders in most fonts)


RULE 11 — SPACING, SCRIPT DIRECTION & REPETITION MARK

  a) SPACING: Spaces separate PHRASES or CLAUSES — NOT individual words.
     Words within a phrase are written continuously with no spaces.
     ✗ Wrong: ຂ້ອຍ ໄປ ຕະຫຼາດ ມື້ ນີ້
     ✓ Correct: ຂ້ອຍໄປຕະຫຼາດມື້ນີ້

  b) REPETITION MARK ໆ: Appended immediately after the word — NO preceding space.
     ✗ Wrong: ດີ ໆ / ໄວ ໆ
     ✓ Correct: ດີໆ / ໄວໆ / ຄ່ອຍໆ

  c) Script runs left to right. No case distinction. No conjunct consonant forms.


RULE 12 — POST-1975 ORTHOGRAPHY REFORM (MoE STANDARD)

The 1975 reform replaced the Pali-based orthography with a phonologically
transparent system. Always use post-1975 reformed spelling:
  • Do NOT use ຣ where modern Lao uses ລ or ຮ
  • Do NOT add silent consonants that Thai retained but Lao reform eliminated
  • Do NOT use pre-reform duplicate consonant forms


RULE 13 — REGISTER SYSTEM

  • FORMAL / OFFICIAL (ທາງການ): Government, legal, EIA/technical reports,
    ministerial correspondence → ຂ້ານ້ອຍ / ທ່ານ, full sentences, ແດ່ for requests.
  • SEMI-FORMAL / PROFESSIONAL (ສຸພາບ): Business emails, proposals, announcements.
  • CASUAL / COLLOQUIAL (ກັນເອງ): Personal messages, social media → ຂ້ອຍ / ເຈົ້າ,
    pronouns may be dropped.
  • TECHNICAL (ວິຊາການ): Sector-specific documents → established Lao technical
    terms; transliterate foreign terms only when no Lao equivalent exists.

  DEFAULT: If context is ambiguous → Semi-Formal/Professional.
